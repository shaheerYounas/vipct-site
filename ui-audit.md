# UI and UX Audit

This audit reviews the website as a premium transfer-company marketing experience. The current visual direction has useful ingredients: dark background, gold accent, fleet imagery, sticky header, and direct WhatsApp contact. The main problem is that the experience feels assembled page-by-page instead of designed as a single high-trust booking funnel.

## UI Issues

| Issue | Where It Appears | Why It Matters | Severity | Exact Fix | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Homepage hero does not visually prove the VIP transfer service | `index.html:39` to `index.html:57`; background in `assets/style.css:56` | The first viewport says "luxury transfers" but gives limited proof: no real fleet detail, no driver/service cues, no review proof, no airport process. | High | Replace the current card-like hero with a stronger above-the-fold section: premium vehicle image, headline, 2 CTAs, trust strip, and route examples. Use copy such as "Private Prague airport and Europe transfers" plus "Fixed quote in 10 minutes". | Better first impression, stronger trust, higher CTA engagement. |
| Homepage service and fleet sections are text-only cards | `index.html:60` to `index.html:85` | VIP/luxury services need visual confirmation. Plain cards make the site feel static and unfinished. | High | Convert service cards into image cards using service photos, short benefit copy, and one CTA per row. Add fleet images in the fleet preview rather than text-only cards. | More premium feel and clearer service discovery. |
| Production placeholder copy is visible | `programs.html:23` | "Tip: later we can add pages..." makes the site look unfinished and reduces trust. | Critical | Remove internal note. Replace with customer-facing copy: "Private day trips from Prague with professional driver, flexible schedule, and comfortable vehicles." | Immediate credibility improvement. |
| Header markup is malformed on homepage | `index.html:13` to `index.html:20` | The `.logo` div is not closed correctly before the brand text. This can affect layout and accessibility. | High | Close the logo container after the logo image: `<div class="logo"><img ... alt="VIP Coach Transfers"></div><div>...</div>`. | More predictable header layout and cleaner HTML. |
| Navigation has no mobile-specific pattern | `.nav`, `.links`, `.lang` in `assets/style.css:27` to `assets/style.css:54` | On small screens, nav links wrap and compete with the logo and language switch. This can crowd the first viewport. | High | Add mobile CSS: horizontal scroll nav or a menu button. Ensure quote and WhatsApp remain visible. | Better mobile usability and fewer lost booking clicks. |
| CTA text is generic | CTAs in `index.html:46`, `services.html:153`, `fleet.html:222`, `quote.html:132`, `contact.html:74` | "Get a Quote" is functional but not persuasive. VIP transfer users need speed, fixed pricing, and confidence. | Medium | Use stronger CTA copy: "Get fixed price", "Check availability", "Book on WhatsApp", "Request airport pickup". Keep primary CTA consistent. | Higher click-through rate from landing pages. |
| Contact page uses a centered information card followed by oversized map | `contact.html:40` to `contact.html:90` | Contact details are readable, but the page does not prioritize immediate action or reassure the visitor about response time. | Medium | Create a two-column contact section: left contact actions, right company details. Move map below at 360-420px height. Add "Usually replies within minutes on WhatsApp". | Faster user action and less scroll friction. |
| Quote form feels like a raw admin form | `quote.html:43` to `quote.html:139` | It asks for many fields in one column with little guidance. Users may not know what is required for an accurate quote. | High | Group the form into sections: Trip, Passengers and vehicle, Contact, Notes. Add helper text and a summary panel. Keep WhatsApp available after minimum required fields. | Lower form abandonment and better lead quality. |
| Return trip fields appear abruptly | `quote.html:68` to `quote.html:79`; logic in `quote.html:230` to `quote.html:241` | Showing/hiding fields works, but there is no visual grouping or transition, so the form can feel jumpy. | Low | Wrap return fields in a titled section with a small note. Use CSS class toggles instead of inline `display:none`. | More polished form behavior. |
| WhatsApp modal is styled entirely inline | `quote.html:153` to `quote.html:186` | Inline modal styles are hard to maintain and inconsistent with other components. | Medium | Move modal styles into `assets/components.css` with classes such as `.modalOverlay`, `.modal`, `.modalHeader`, `.bookingPreview`. | Cleaner code and easier visual consistency. |
| Language behavior is inconsistent across pages | `services.html:94` to `services.html:97`; `assets/app.js:41` to `assets/app.js:70` | Services page uses language blocks, other pages use `data-i18n`. The content model is mixed. | Medium | Standardize multilingual rendering: either full translated static pages or complete `data-i18n` dictionaries. Avoid mixing approaches per section. | More predictable translations and easier content updates. |
| Programs page has broken image references and missing alt text | `programs.html:34` to `programs.html:60` | Broken or inaccessible cards reduce polish and harm accessibility. | High | Fix file names, add descriptive `alt`, and add `loading="lazy"` and dimensions. | Restores visual quality and improves accessibility. |
| The visual system uses many similar dark cards | `.card` in `assets/style.css:82`; many pages | Repeated dark cards make all sections feel equal. There is not enough hierarchy between hero, proof, services, quote, and footer. | Medium | Add section types: hero, trust strip, service list, fleet showcase, review band, FAQ band. Use cards only for repeated items. | Stronger page rhythm and clearer user path. |
| Trust signals are too light | Homepage badges in `index.html:50` to `index.html:55`; contact details in `contact.html:46` to `contact.html:70` | VIP transfer buyers need proof before sharing trip details. | High | Add review rating, company registration, insured/licensed message, 24/7 support, flight tracking, driver professionalism, and real fleet proof near CTAs. | More confidence and more quote starts. |

## Page-Specific Recommendations

| Page | Recommended Changes | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage | Build around hero, trust strip, popular routes, fleet preview with images, airport transfer process, testimonials, FAQ, final CTA. | Turns the homepage into a conversion landing page instead of a directory. |
| Services | Fix image paths, reduce long accordion-like Arabic-only detail, add service cards with benefit bullets and CTAs, add FAQs. | Makes service selection clearer and supports SEO. |
| Fleet | Keep filters, fix missing hero image, add actual capacity/luggage comparison table, add "best for" labels. | Helps users choose a vehicle and submit better requests. |
| Programs | Remove placeholder text, fix broken images, add destination cards with duration, ideal season, vehicle options, and CTA. | Makes programs bookable and less like a draft page. |
| Quote | Redesign as guided form with route presets, vehicle recommendation, response promise, and summary. | Reduces friction and increases completed leads. |
| Contact | Prioritize WhatsApp and phone, then company details, then map. | Faster contact and higher mobile conversion. |
